Cookie Policy

How Retro Replay UK uses cookies and similar technologies, and how you can control them.

Quick summary

  • We use cookies to help the site work properly and to understand how visitors use the site.
  • Google Analytics (Google tag) may set analytics cookies to measure traffic and usage.
  • You can control cookies via your browser settings. You can also opt out of Google Analytics.
  • Blocking some cookies may impact site features.

Last updated: 05 January 2026

1) What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They are commonly used to make websites work efficiently, to improve user experience, and to provide reporting information.

Some cookies are set by the website you’re visiting (“first-party cookies”). Others may be set by third-party services embedded on the website (“third-party cookies”).

2) Why we use cookies

  • Essential: to help core site functions operate correctly.
  • Analytics: to understand site traffic and improve the site.
  • Functionality: to remember choices where applicable (if enabled).

Note: This site does not intentionally use cookies to build advertising profiles. If this changes, this policy will be updated.

3) Cookies and services we use

The cookies on this website may include the following categories. The exact names and lifetimes can vary over time as providers update their services.

Essential (site)
• Used to support basic site behaviour (may include security or preference cookies if implemented).

Analytics (Google Analytics / Google tag)
• Used to measure visits, page views, and interactions so we can improve the site.
• These cookies may be set by Google when the Google tag is present on the page.

If you would like a precise, current cookie list (name, provider, expiry), you can generate one using a cookie scanner tool after deploying the site (because cookies can differ by browser/device and may only appear after interactions).

4) Managing cookies in your browser

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can usually:

  • Delete existing cookies
  • Block all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies only
  • Set preferences per website

If you disable cookies, some site functionality may not work as expected.

5) Opting out of Google Analytics

Google provides a browser add-on that can be used to opt out of Google Analytics tracking across websites that use it.

Search for: Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on

6) Do Not Track

Some browsers include a “Do Not Track” (DNT) setting. There is no universal standard for how websites respond to DNT signals. Where possible, we aim to respect privacy-focused browser features. If you require strict blocking, use browser cookie controls and/or content-blocking tools.

7) Changes to this Cookie Policy

We may update this policy from time to time to reflect changes to the site or legal requirements. Any updates will be posted on this page with an updated “Last updated” date.

8) Contact

If you have questions about this Cookie Policy, contact us via the website contact page.
