Cookie Policy

This page explains what cookies are, which cookies RetroReplay UK uses, and how you can manage your preferences.

Quick summary: We use cookies to measure traffic and improve the website. We never sell personal data.

What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help websites function, remember preferences, and (optionally) collect anonymous usage statistics.

Cookies we use

  • Essential cookies — required for functionality (e.g., remembering cookie consent).
  • Analytics cookies — optional, used for anonymous traffic measurement (Google Analytics).

We keep cookies minimal and privacy-focused. This page is updated whenever tools change.

Your choices

You can accept or reject analytics cookies using our on-site cookie banner or the button below.

You can also manage cookies via your browser’s privacy settings.

Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics to measure usage and improve the site. Data collected is anonymised and never used to personally identify you.

  • Pages visited and actions taken
  • Approximate region (IP anonymised)
  • Device/browser type
  • Referral source (e.g. search, social, direct)

You can opt out by rejecting analytics cookies or installing Google’s browser add-on.

Managing cookies in your browser

  • View or delete stored cookies
  • Block cookies from specific sites
  • Block third-party cookies

Blocking all cookies may affect website performance, but RetroReplay.uk remains usable if analytics are rejected.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated occasionally, for example when we add new tools or change analytics settings. The revision date will reflect updates.
